Mix-Net Protocol

We are currently working on a paper detailing both the mix-net and a self-contained proof of security of the particular variation of a reencryption mix-net that we have implemented, as well as a self-contained definition of universally verifiable proofs of correctness that are optionally produced as the result of an execution. For now, we only give a short bullet list of the protocols we use:

  • Joint key generation. This is done by direct use of Feldman, A practical scheme for non-interactive verifiable secret sharing, 28th FOCS. (As explained in Gennaro, Jarecki, Krawczyk, Rabin, Secure Distributed Key Generation for Discrete-Log Based Cryptosystems, Journal of Cryptology 2007, this gives a slightly biased key, but this works fine.)
  • Joint Coin-Flipping. This is done by the standard share-and-recover protocol based on Pedersen, Non-interactive and information-theoretic secure verifiable secret sharing, Crypto '91.
  • Mix-net. We use the reencryption mix-net approach introduced by Park, Itoh, and Kurosawa, Efficient Anonymous Channel and All/Nothing Election Scheme, Eurocrypt '93, but our own proof of a shuffle.
  • Proofs of shuffles. We use the recent proof of a shuffle of Terelius and Wikström, Proofs of Restricted Shuffles, Africacrypt 2010, as well as the precomputation technique from Wikström, Commitment-Consistent Proofs of Shuffles, ACISP 2009.
  • Verifiable Decryption. We use the batching technique of Bellare, Garay, and Rabin, Batch Verification with Applications to Cryptography and Checking, LATIN '98, to speed up the proofs needed for verifiable decryption.
  • Overall proof of security. The definition is taken from Wikström, A Universally Composable Mix-Net, TCC '04, and the analysis is similar to that in Wikström, A Sender Verifiable Mix-Net and a New Proof of a Shuffle, Asiacrypt '05.
  • Universal Verifiability. The protocols optionally use Fiat-Shamir non-interactive proofs based on SHA-256.
